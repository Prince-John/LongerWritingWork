First Derivatives with Memes

Longer writing work for ENGL 262
by Prince John, Spring 2021

More about the title

The title is a play on the mathematical concept of a derivative from calculus. A first derivative of any continuous function, say f(x), can be represented by f’(x). f’(x) gives us the slope of the tangent of function f(x) at x.
The first derivative is often taken with respect to time and that tells us the rate at which the function is changing. For example, if s(t) is a function that represents the speed of a car for any time t the first derivative of this function, s’(t) will be the acceleration of the car. The first derivative will tell us how fast the speed of the car is changing.

For this project I wanted to focus on influences of information exchange on cultural and societal change. This posed a problem as all cultures change over time even without influences of technology, if we analyse the rate of this change or the first derivative of societal change we can get a much better picture of the influences of information technology. To quantify this change I have chose to use memes the way they were originally defined by Richard Dawkins.
